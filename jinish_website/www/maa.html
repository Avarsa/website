Diet and Lifestyle Guidance for Jayasree

This document is based on available scientific research and publicly accessible medical and nutritional data. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice from your doctor.

Understanding Blood Report Parameters

  • Triglycerides (TG): High levels increase heart disease risk. Should ideally be below 150 mg/dL.
  • LDL Cholesterol: 'Bad cholesterol'—can lead to arterial blockages. Keep it under 100 mg/dL.
  • HDL Cholesterol: 'Good cholesterol'—protects your heart. Higher values (above 50 mg/dL) are good.
  • Total Cholesterol: Should be under 200 mg/dL for heart safety.
  • VLDL: Transports TG in the blood. Higher levels indicate higher TG and more risk.

Recommended Diet (with Timing)

  • Early Morning (6:30–7:00 am): Warm water with lemon OR soaked methi seeds (good for lipid metabolism)
  • Breakfast (8:00–8:30 am): Oats with fruits, or red rice idli with sambar (avoid coconut chutney)
  • Mid-morning (10:30 am): 1 fruit (apple, papaya, orange, pear – avoid banana)
  • Lunch (12:30–1:00 pm): Red rice or millets, 1 bowl sambar, vegetables (no frying), salad, 1 tsp olive oil
  • Evening (4:00–5:00 pm): Buttermilk or green tea, roasted chana or sprouts
  • Dinner (7:00–8:00 pm): Same as lunch or vegetable soup with 1 roti (multigrain)

Recommended Oils and Fats

  • Olive Oil (Extra Virgin): High in MUFA, reduces LDL and increases HDL
  • Groundnut Oil: Good alternative, high in MUFA
  • Rice Bran Oil: Contains oryzanol, helps reduce cholesterol
  • Mustard Oil: Anti-inflammatory and heart-friendly, especially good for Indian cooking
  • AVOID: Coconut oil, palm oil, vanaspati, dalda, reused oil

Kerala-specific Food Tips

  • Replace coconut oil with olive or mustard oil for daily use
  • Limit avial and thoran that use grated coconut. Use vegetables steamed or boiled
  • Use red rice, but control portion (1 cup cooked)

Scientific Backing

  • MUFA (Monounsaturated Fatty Acids): Found in olive oil, helps reduce LDL, raises HDL. [Ref: AHA]
  • Fiber-rich foods: Lower cholesterol and improve digestion. [Ref: NIH]
  • Flavonoids in fruits: Help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation. [Ref: Harvard Med]

റിപ്പോർട്ടിലെ പ്രധാന പഠിപ്പുകൾ

  • ട്രൈഗ്ലിസറൈഡുകൾ: ഹൃദ്രോഗ സാധ്യത വർദ്ധിപ്പിക്കുന്നു, 150 mg/dL ൽ താഴെയാകണം.
  • LDL കൊളസ്‌ട്രോൾ: 'ചീത്ത കൊളസ്‌ട്രോൾ' ആണ്, കുറയ്ക്കേണ്ടതാണ്.
  • HDL കൊളസ്‌ട്രോൾ: 'നല്ല കൊളസ്‌ട്രോൾ', വർദ്ധിപ്പിക്കുക.
  • VLDL: ട്രൈഗ്ലിസറൈഡുകൾ ശരീരത്തിൽ വിതരണം ചെയ്യുന്നു, കുറച്ചിരിക്കുക.
